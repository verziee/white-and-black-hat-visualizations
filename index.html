Data Visualization Ethics

White Hat vs Black Hat: Police Complaint Trends

This interactive comparison demonstrates how the same data can be shown honestly (white hat visualization) or manipulatively (black hat visualization). This website includes two examples from the NYPD complaint records that show how data can be visualized in a misleading manner. The two examples highlighted are department-wide complaint substantiation rates and officer specific complaint rates, both of which can be accessed via the dropdown menu below.

Black Hat

Cherry-picked years with a narrow axis to inflate the trend.

Misleading

This chart selects only the most recent years and zooms into a narrow 66–76% range. That makes a subtle decline look dramatic and encourages the viewer to think lower substantiation is clearly better.

Substantiated NYPD Officer Complaints Significantly Decreasing

What substantiation means

  • Substantiation rate = complaints found valid by the review process.
  • A lower rate is often framed as positive because it suggests fewer validated misconduct cases.
  • That interpretation is only fair if the data and axis are not manipulated.

Why the black hat is misleading

  • It shows only recent years rather than the full trend.
  • It zooms into a narrow percentage range to exaggerate small change.
  • It implies a normal decline is a strong improvement, even though the full story is slimmer.

Data source: NYPD complaint dataset. Note: substantiation rate reflects investigation outcomes, not proof of wrongdoing.
